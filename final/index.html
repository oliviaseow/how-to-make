1. What does it do?

Touch or remotely trigger the blooming and color changing of a martian marigold and generate mutant flora on one solar punk device.

The title of my project is inspired by a book, The Effect of Gamma Rays on Man-in-the-Moon Marigolds in which Tillie posits that gamma rays on the moon can sometimes cause good mutations on marigolds.

My SMA flower has outerspace alien-y vibes, and mutant flora GAN can be generated by sliding your finger across on-device slider.

2. My contribution

Nitinol SMA is notoriously difficult to work with. Short of access to precision engineering machines, it was advised that we stay away from this material. However, I really wanted to achive the organic motion without external pulleys or motors. Through many hours of persistance, I found a way to train the wire to behave in a desirable way without using an industrial oven.

I also found a unique way to fill and color the empty space in an SMA petal such that its movement is not restricted. Moreover, my method allows us to use the heat generated by the SMA to trigger a thermochromic color change.

5. Reflections and future improvements

I am quite pleased with what I achieved given the time that I had. The flower has an outer-worldly movement, and the slider works very well.

  1. The pink nail polish from Sally Hansen beads up when applied to SMA and looks pretty bad. I want to find a better (and easier) way to enamel the SMA in the future.
  2. The spacing of the box could be reevaluated to allow people to interact with the slider without interferring with the flower
  3. The colors of the petals could be further experimented with.
  4. The planet on the case did not fit very well, and had to be externally affixed. In the future, I would design a locking mechanism to do this more cleanly.
